Q12,12.1, 12.2 - Is the inspection summary complete and consistent with the rest of the inspection report?

Q12,12.1, 12.2 - Is the inspection summary complete and consistent with the rest of the inspection report?

[For some DBFO structures the inspection summary may be on the Inspection Report Document and may not appear in the inspection summary field within IAMIS. Where there is no requirement for an inspection summary, such as a BE11 Inspection and there is no attached inspection report, the score should be 5.]

Grade your agreement with this statement from 1(strongly disagree) to 5 (strongly agree). A guide to grading is as follows.

5 – Matches all requirements of the guidance and is fully consistent with the rest of the report.

4 – Minor, cosmetic defects not mentioned

3 – Some minor inconsistencies, such as maintenance actions not matching up or interim measures not commented on.

2 – Defects with repair recommendations not mentioned in the Summary

1 – Summary missing or major inconsistencies

The inspection summary should be consistent with all other parts of the inspection report. If a defect or maintenance requirement is identified in the inspection summary, it should also be identified, with matching details in the ‘Observations/Defects Confirmed at this Inspection’ section of the report, likewise, if a significant defect, requiring repairs, is observed and recorded during the inspection, it should be listed in the ‘Inspection Summary’.

SMIS user manuals gave guidance on this field.

Inspection SummaryThis should give a representative overview of the structure condition in relation to its construction type, age and usage. Defects, which are or may affect the structural integrity and / or the durability of the structure, should be specifically identified. Defects, which affect appearance, only should be briefly summarised. Significant proposed Maintenance Actions should be identified.

Q14 - Have any significant elements been recorded as not inspected?

Q14 - Have any significant elements been recorded as not inspected?

Answer Yes/No

Q19 - Is there sufficient evidence to support the severity / extent score assigned to the defects?

Q19 - Is there sufficient evidence to support the severity / extent score assigned to the defects?

View the information within the inspection report and use your judgement to grade your agreement with the statement above from 5 (strongly agree) to 1 (strongly disagree). A guide to grading is as follows.

Note: If no defects are present and/or the report is a BE11 Inspection, then the response shall be 5.

5 – All (100%) defects identified within the report are clearly shown in photographs (and/or sketches), and all (100%) defect severity / extent ratings appear to be correct

4 – Most (between 75% and 100%) defects identified within the report are clearly shown in photographs (and/or sketches), and most (between 75% and 100%) defect severity / extent ratings appear to be correct.

3 – One of the following applies:

A significant proportion (between 25% and 50%) of defects are not clearly shown in photographs (and/or sketches)

Or

A significant proportion (between 25% and 50%) of defect severity / extent ratings appear to be erroneous.

2 – One of the following applies:

The majority (between 50% and 100%) of defects are not clearly shown in photographs (and/or sketches)

Or

The majority (between 50% and 100%) of defect severity / extent ratings appear to be erroneous.

1 – Both of the following apply:

The majority (between 50% and 100%) of defects are not clearly shown in photographs (and/or sketches)

And

The majority (between 50% and 100%) of defect severity / extent ratings appear to be erroneous.

If your response is between 1 and 3 then you will be prompted to provide an explanation describing what the deficiencies were. Please provide specific examples with reference to the inspection.

Q. 23 - Any further comments?

Q. 23 - Any further comments?

Please use this space to record any further information you think has not been covered, that is relevant to the quality of the inspection report. Please ensure that any statements made are backed up with specific examples from the inspection.

Note: When reviewing inspections significant issues may became apparent that would not be captured by specific questions within the template. These issues may not always be related to the quality of the inspection report. For example, a bridge may have no interim measures in place but the recorded capacity below 40 Tonnes Assessment Live Loading. It is important that such issues are not ignored, and any such issues identified in the course of undertaking the review should be captured in the ‘Further Comments’ box.
